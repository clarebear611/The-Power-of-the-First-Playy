THE ORIGIN OF CHESS

600 AD - 2026

Chess began in India around the 6th century during the Gupta Empire. The early version of the game was called chaturanga which means “four divisions of the military”. The four are infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots. These military units later evolved into modern pawns, knights, bishops, and rooks. This game was played on an 8x8 board and was designed to test strategy.

From India, the game spread to Persia where it was named shatranj. When the Islamic Empire expanded, the game traveled across the Middle East, North Africa, and into Europe. By the late 1400s, the rules changed to make the game faster and more powerful — especially the queen, who became the strongest piece.

THE FOUR PLAYS

E4

Described by Bobby Fischer as “best by test.” It is the most popular opening and has been used for centuries.

D4

A strategic opening focused on long-term positional play. It became popular in the 19th century.

C4

A flank opening popularized by Howard Staunton. Widely used by world champions.

Nf3

The Reti Opening. A flexible move that develops the knight and prepares control of the center.
